Palm Springs Weekend Getaway

Palm Springs is the perfect desert escape — warm sunshine, mid-century charm, world-class food, and a pace of life that's made for unwinding. This guide is tailored for a relaxed, mama-friendly weekend with plenty of shade, great bites, leisurely strolls, and quality pool time. Your Australian Shepherd is welcome at nearly every turn — Palm Springs is one of the most dog-friendly cities in California.

Shopping

Uptown Design District

View on Google Maps

The Uptown Design District, running along North Palm Canyon Drive, is the more curated, gallery-forward end of Palm Springs. The pace is slower, the shops are more considered, and it's where you'll find the boutiques best suited to a clean, minimal aesthetic. Extremely dog-friendly — water bowls appear outside shops and your Aussie will be a regular fixture of the sidewalk scene.

Wolf Contemporary [Map]

A must-stop for handbag lovers. Owner Mark Rose has spent 40 years curating archival designer pieces — think rare Céline, Bottega Veneta, and collectible jewelry. It's as much gallery as boutique. 768 N. Palm Canyon Drive.

Elizabeth & Prince [Map]

A quiet, beautifully edited lifestyle boutique whose ethos — "simplicity and nature as the new luxury" — maps directly onto a Row or Sézane sensibility. Clean silhouettes, quality fabrics, no noise.

preLoved Palm Springs [Map]

Curated vintage designer women's clothing — worth a browse for deadstock finds and pre-owned pieces from elevated labels.

The Uptown District is also dense with art galleries — a pleasant excuse to slow down, step inside, and get out of the sun. Businesses along the whole stretch put out water bowls for dogs.

El Paseo Shopping District

Palm Desert, about a 20-minute drive.  View on Google Maps

Called the "Rodeo Drive of the Desert" for good reason — El Paseo is a beautifully shaded, flat mile of upscale retail anchored by the only Saks Fifth Avenue within 90 miles of Palm Springs. For someone who loves The Row, Céline, and Sézane, this is where the serious shopping happens. Many shops welcome dogs inside.

Bottega Veneta [Map]

The standout for handbags — the intrecciato weave and quiet-luxury minimalism aligns perfectly with a Céline or Row sensibility. A destination stop.

Saks Fifth Avenue [Map]

At The Gardens on El Paseo. The anchor store and the most reliable place to find contemporary luxury labels including The Row, Céline accessories, and similar clean, investment-piece brands under one roof.

Louis Vuitton [Map]

A full boutique at The Gardens on El Paseo — a natural stop for handbag browsing.

Gucci [Map]

Also in the district — worth a look for their understated leather goods and accessories.

The Gardens on El Paseo [Map]

A beautiful open-air mall within the district — perfect for a mid-shop sit-down and cold drink.

Dogs are welcome along the entire El Paseo promenade and in many shops. Check if the First Friday El Paseo Art Walk is happening during your trip (November through May) — a lovely evening event, and dogs are very welcome.

Relaxing & Things to Do

Pool Time at the Airbnb

Floating in a private desert pool with mountain views is one of the quintessential Palm Springs experiences. Plan around the heat — mornings and late afternoons are ideal. Evenings by the pool as the sun sets behind the San Jacinto Mountains are magical. Australian Shepherds are herding dogs with high energy and many love the water, so the pool can double as enrichment time for your pup too.

The desert sun is intense — always have sunscreen, a wide-brim hat, and cold drinks nearby. A pool float or inflatable lounger makes the experience extra dreamy. Sunrise dips are peaceful and cool — worth setting an alarm for at least one morning. Aussies tend to love swimming — let your pup join you for some supervised water play and rinse them off with fresh water after.

Village Fest

Thursday evenings on Palm Canyon Drive.  View on Google Maps

If your trip overlaps with a Thursday, the weekly Village Fest street fair is a delight. Local vendors, street food, art, and live music in a festive but relaxed atmosphere. It is one of the most dog-friendly events in Palm Springs — bring the Aussie and enjoy the people-watching together.

Moorten Botanical Garden

View on Google Maps

A charming, small cactus garden and plant museum that's been a Palm Springs staple since 1938. A shaded, slow-paced wander through desert flora — beautiful for an hour-long visit. Leashed dogs are welcome in the garden, making it a great gentle outing for the whole family.

Palm Springs Aerial Tramway

Optional scenic detour.  View on Google Maps

A short drive from the city center, the Tram ascends to 8,500 feet in the San Jacinto Mountains with breathtaking views. Worth it if you feel up for it — but note that dogs are not permitted on the Aerial Tramway (service animals excepted). Plan this as a solo adult excursion and arrange for the pup to stay cool at the Airbnb.

Cafés & Coffee Spots

Palm Springs has an excellent independent coffee scene. These spots are all welcoming, beautifully designed, and perfect for a slow morning or afternoon break. Most have patio or outdoor seating where your Aussie can join you.

Koffi [Map]

The Palm Springs institution. Several locations around town, each with a gorgeous outdoor patio. Excellent espresso, smoothies, and a laid-back local vibe. Dogs are welcome on the patio — a morning ritual with your Aussie by your side is hard to beat.

Coffeeism Co. [Map]

A family-owned café in the heart of downtown with a Scandinavian-minimalist feel. Craft coffee, a calm atmosphere, and a beautiful interior. Outdoor seating available for you and your pup.

La Jefa Cafe [Map]

A Latin-influenced coffee house and restaurant with stunning interior and cute outdoor seating. One of the most photogenic spots in town — grab an outside table and make a morning of it.

Gré Coffeehouse [Map]

Tucked into a courtyard off Palm Canyon Drive — part café, part record shop, part art gallery. A wonderful, eclectic spot for a mid-morning pause with outdoor courtyard seating.

Ernest Coffee [Map]

Named after the founder of tiki culture, Ernest Gantt. Serves Stumptown Coffee in a laid-back, welcoming space with outdoor seating. A great all-day spot where your Aussie is a welcome addition.

Cartel Coffee Lab [Map]

Inside the stylish Arrive Hotel — a standout craft coffee bar. Great for a mid-afternoon treat, with patio seating in the hotel's gorgeous courtyard.

Where to Eat

Palm Springs' dining scene is exceptional for a city its size — a mix of Mexican classics, California farm-to-table, upscale weekend treats, and beloved local institutions. Dog-friendly patio dining is a genuine specialty of the city, so you'll have plenty of options to dine with your Aussie in tow.

Casual & Everyday

Sherman's Deli & Bakery [Map]

A Palm Springs institution since 1963 — New York deli brought to the desert. The Reuben sandwich, housemade matzo ball soup, and giant corned beef plate are all essential. Great for carry-out. Perfect for a lazy pool lunch — order big portions and graze.

Wilma & Frieda [Map]

Beloved local brunch spot in Mercado Plaza with pretty courtyard seating. Eggs Benedict, avocado toast, and fresh-squeezed juices. Almost always busy — arrive early or expect a short wait. Worth it.

Farm [Map]

Health-forward California cuisine using fresh, seasonal local produce. Seasonal grain bowls, roasted veggie plates, and fresh salads. Perfect for a light, nourishing lunch. Great carry-out option.

El Mirasol Cocina Mexicana [Map]

Authentic, vibrant Mexican with a warm atmosphere and fresh ingredients. The tacos, enchiladas, and fresh-made guacamole are all wonderful. Great sit-down or carry-out — one of the best traditional Mexican spots in the area.

Mid-Range & Buzzy

Clandestino [Map]

Moody, stylish elevated Mexican near the Marilyn Monroe statue downtown. The stuffed squash blossoms and cochinita pibil are standouts. Perfect for a date-night dinner without being too formal.

The Pink Cabana [Map]

At the Sands Hotel & Spa. Moroccan-influenced menu in a vibrant, lush setting that's absolutely stunning. Book a table on the terrace if possible.

Ash & Vine [Map]

Unpretentious elegance from Chef Matthew Resler. The grilled cheese dumplings and filet with caramelized cauliflower are signature dishes. One of the best newer openings in town. Intimate and delicious.

The Restaurant at PS Surf Club [Map]

Reopened February 2025. Surf-inspired California menu led by Chef Michael Hung — sea bass in banana leaf, tuna melt on sourdough, crispy wagyu cigars. A great casual-upscale dinner choice.

Special Occasion & Upscale

Bar Cecil [Map]

Michelin Guide recognized. Paying homage to Cecil Beaton — chic and exceptional. Deviled eggs with caviar, seasonal tasting menu, classic cocktails. Book in advance. A memorable splurge dinner for the two of you.

Le Vallauris [Map]

Iconic since 1974. Set in a historic 1927 villa with lush garden terrace dining. Classic French bistro plates and a fresh seasonal tasting menu. One of the most romantic settings in Palm Springs.

Bar Issi [Map]

Inside the Thompson Hotel. Coastal Italian sharing plates, pastas, and Aperol-forward cocktails in a maximalist, photogenic setting. Great for a fun, scenic dinner.

Dog-Friendly Patio Dining

These spots are specifically known for welcoming dogs — bring your Aussie along for the meal.

Boozehounds [Map]

Japanese-influenced handcrafted food and cocktails in a fun outdoor atrium setting. Creative small plates and craft cocktails. Has a dedicated dog menu — dogs are the whole point of the name.

Bongo Johnny's [Map]

Lively patio bar with a large shaded outdoor space perfect for lingering. Burgers, sandwiches, and classic bar food. Offers a special Woof! Dog Menu — your Aussie eats too.

Blue Coyote [Map]

Just before the Uptown Design District — a rambling maze of shaded outdoor dining. Salads, burgers, and Southwestern plates. Massive dog-friendly patio with plenty of room for an energetic Aussie.

Lulu California Bistro [Map]

Modern California cuisine in the heart of downtown on a spacious patio. Brunch plates, fresh salads, and California comfort food. Dogs welcome at all outdoor patio tables — centrally located for easy walks.

Brunch 101 [Map]

A welcoming brunch spot where pups are as pampered as their owners. Brunch classics, egg dishes, and mimosas. The patio is explicitly dog-friendly and laid-back — great lazy morning spot.

Don't Miss

Date Shake — a Palm Springs staple made with locally grown Coachella Valley dates. Try one at any local date farm shop or grocery. An absolute must. Kreem serves artisanal ice cream using organic, natural flavors with vegan options — the perfect afternoon treat.

El Jefe at the Saguaro [Map]

Fun Mexican street food and a lively happy hour atmosphere. Dog-friendly outdoor space.

Bringing Your Australian Shepherd

Australian Shepherds are smart, active, and social dogs who thrive with engagement. The desert heat requires some extra planning, but Palm Springs is genuinely one of California's most dog-welcoming cities. With a few adjustments to your timing and routine, your Aussie will have a fantastic trip.

Desert Heat & Paw Safety

This is the most important consideration for your Aussie. The desert ground — pavement, asphalt, sand — can get scorching hot and burn paw pads quickly. Use the 7-second rule: press the back of your hand to the pavement, and if you can't hold it there for 7 seconds, it's too hot for your dog's paws.

Walk your Aussie before 9am or after 5pm to avoid peak ground heat. Consider bringing dog booties or paw wax like Musher's Secret for any midday outings. Australian Shepherds have thick double coats — always carry a collapsible water bowl and offer fresh water every 15 to 20 minutes outdoors. Watch for signs of overheating: heavy panting, drooling, glassy eyes, or stumbling. Find shade and cool water immediately if you notice these signs.

Dog Parks

Give your Aussie a proper run — these local dog parks are well-maintained and a great way to burn off that herding breed energy.

David H. Ready Palm Springs Dog Park [Map]

Near City Hall, centrally located. Fenced with separate large and small dog areas, water fountains, and benches. Great for a morning romp.

Rancho Mirage Dog Park [Map]

About 20 minutes away but worth it. Locals' favorite, with ample shaded seating and well-maintained turf. Pair with an El Paseo shopping trip nearby.

Pool Time with Your Aussie

Australian Shepherds were bred to work around farms and water, and many absolutely love to swim. A private Airbnb pool is a perfect place for supervised water play. Most Aussies take naturally to water — let your pup join you for a dip with supervision. Rinse your dog off with fresh water after pool time to remove chlorine from their coat, and keep a fresh water bowl poolside so they're drinking clean water rather than pool water. Mental stimulation matters for Aussies — if the pool isn't a hit, bring a toy or puzzle feeder to keep them settled while you float.

Practical Tips

A few things to keep in mind for a comfortable trip with a pregnant mama and an Australian Shepherd in the desert.

  • Plan outdoor activities — walks, shopping, dog park runs — before 11am or after 4pm. The midday desert sun is fierce for people and pups alike.
  • Always carry water. The dry desert air is dehydrating, especially important when pregnant. Pack a separate collapsible bowl and bottle just for your Aussie.
  • Most restaurants and cafés are walkable from a central Airbnb, or a very short ride away. For dog-friendly dining, Boozehounds, Bongo Johnny's, Lulu California Bistro, and Blue Coyote are all downtown and easy to reach on foot with the dog.
  • Make reservations at Bar Cecil and Le Vallauris in advance — they fill up, especially on weekends. Plan these as "just the two of you" dinners while the Aussie relaxes at the Airbnb.
  • El Paseo in Palm Desert is worth the 20-minute drive for a shopping half-day. Swing by the Rancho Mirage Dog Park on the same trip to let your Aussie run.
  • Sherman's Deli and Farm are the top picks for carry-out pool lunches — order generously, eat in the shade, and let the dog lounge nearby.
  • The Aerial Tramway does not allow dogs — plan this without your pup, or skip it for a fully dog-inclusive trip.
Have an incredible trip — enjoy every slow, sunny, delicious moment.
